Muhammad Al-Khwarizmi

The Father of Algebra

Here's a timeline of Al-Khwarizmi's life:

  • 780 CE - Born in Khwarazm (modern-day Uzbekistan), part of the Abbasid Caliphate.
  • 800 CE - Moves to Baghdad, the intellectual heart of the Islamic Golden Age, and becomes part of the House of Wisdom, a renowned academy for scholars.
  • 820 CE - Writes "Kitab al-Mukhtasar fi Hisab al-Jabr wal-Muqabala" (The Compendious Book on Calculation by Completion and Balancing), introducing the foundational principles of algebra. This book gives the field its name: "al-jabr."
  • 825 CE - Authors "Zij al-Sindhind", a comprehensive astronomical and trigonometric text based on Hindu and Greek sources, advancing the study of celestial mechanics.
  • 830 CE - Produces works that popularize the Hindu-Arabic numeral system (including the concept of zero) in the Islamic world, which later influences European mathematics.
  • 840 CE - Writes on geography, revising Ptolemyâ€™s work and creating one of the most accurate maps of the known world at the time. His book, "Kitab Surat al-Ard" (The Image of the Earth), becomes a reference for centuries.
  • 12th century CE - Al-Khwarizmi's works are translated into Latin, introducing his name as "Algoritmi," a term that evolves into "algorithm," essential to modern computer science.
  • Legacy - Al-Khwarizmi's contributions provide the framework for solving quadratic equations and influence fields as diverse as mathematics, astronomy, geography, and engineering.
